Donating Stock Is A Good Idea

Donating stock to charity benefits both the donor and the charity organization. If a donor donates a stock that has increased in value, neither they nor ARCD will pay capital gains tax. What’s more, the donation is tax-deductible if the donor itemizes their taxes. This allows them to claim a deduction based on the value of the stock at the time of transfer (provided that they have owned the stock for at least one year and one day). 

Once the donation is complete, the donor will receive automatically a written confirmation of their contribution for their tax records. 

Considerations for donors:

  • Stocks Owned For At Least One Year

    • Donors obtain a larger tax benefit for stocks donated after the 1-year threshold. Stock sales are subject to capital gains taxes based on how long the investor has held the stock.  If a stock has been held for less than 1 year, the tax benefits are reduced. We encourage donors to donate stocks held for at least one year and avoid donating stocks held for less than one year.

  • Successful Stocks

    • Stocks that have appreciated significantly are great to consider for a charitable contribution because stocks that have increased in value have a large capital gain at the time of sale. Thus, these are good stocks to target for a donation as opposed to stocks that have lost value.

  • Publicly-Traded Stocks

    • For the benefit of ARCD, it’s always best to donate a stock that trades on a public exchange because other stocks or assets are more complex security sales. Privately-held stocks, or restricted shares of public stock, are not ideal for charitable donations.
